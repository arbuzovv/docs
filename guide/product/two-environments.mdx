---
title: "Two Environments: Russia & US"
description: "How the Russian and US environments differ"
---

Ziplime runs as two independent environments, built for two different markets.

<CardGroup cols={2}>
  <Card title="Russian environment" icon="flag" href="https://ziplime.finam.ru/">
    Runs on Yandex Cloud infrastructure. Instruments: stocks, mutual funds, futures, and bonds.
  </Card>

  <Card title="US environment" icon="flag-usa" href="http://ziplime.limex.com">
    Runs on AWS infrastructure. Instruments: ETFs and stocks.
  </Card>
</CardGroup>

The available instruments differ between environments — the Russian environment covers four asset classes, while the US environment covers ETFs and stocks. Don't assume the two are interchangeable.

Technically, the Russian environment is a fork of the US one with added localization, but **the two environments are fully independent**: the US side runs on infrastructure in the US, the Russian side runs on Finam's internal infrastructure. Load, updates, or incidents on one side don't affect the other.
